Kidney disease or Acute renal failure occurs in human beings when these organs have no more ability for a proper function. What is Acute Kidney Disease?

Acute kidney disease is the type of condition in which the kidneys do not have the ability for eliminating excess salts, fluids or waste materials from the blood of the human body. In short, the main function of the kidneys is not done and the kidney loses its ability to filter blood. This is called acute kidney disease, because it is developed in a short span of time, and are common to the people who are already admitted to a hospital or critically ill.

Causes of Acute Kidney Disease

Acute Kidney disease or acute kidney failure can be caused due to many reasons. Most of the common reasons are –

  • Acute Tubular Necrosis (ATN)
  • Sudden dehydration
  • Toxic Kidney Injury which can happen due to poison or any particular medications
  • Auto-immune kidney diseases
  • Urinary Tract Obstruction
  • Reduced blood flow due to low blood pressure, dehydration, serious illness or surgery

Apart from the above cases, a blood clot in the blood vessels of your kidney can also lead to acute kidney failure. This condition can be caused due to reasons such as –

  • Scleroderma
  • Malignant Hypertension
  • Transfusion Reaction
  • Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome
  • Idiopathic Thrombocytopenic Thrombotic Purpura (ITTP)

Few infections, such as acute pyelonephritis and septicemia can also cause acute kidney failure by injuring your kidneys. Some pregnancy conditions can also cause acute kidney disease by harming the kidneys, which includes conditions such as placenta previa and placental abruption.
